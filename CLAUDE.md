# Crew Transition — Project Context

## How to work (always)
- Ask before assuming; if unsure how a request maps to the code, ask.
- Lead with plain-language usability impact (I'm a PM; I won't review code).
- Work incrementally; commit to a review branch and hold for my verdict.
- Never fabricate content (testimonials, numbers); honest placeholder > fake.
- On strategy/product calls, be a critical advisor — stress-test, surface risks, give a clear recommendation; don't just agree.

## Hard rule — engine vs. framing
- DON'T touch the analytical engine: scoring, pathway selection, archetypes, contradiction detection, knowledge docs.
- DO reframe presentation freely: structure, tone, wording, order, CTA.
- If a change could alter what the analysis *concludes*, stop and ask.

## Positioning (governs all copy/UX)
- Worth & curiosity, never escape. Honor the job. Lead with "see what your skills are worth."
- Audience is suppressed-need. Search is the primary channel *for now*; pride/social is the higher-ceiling demand-creation channel, promoted to primary only if it *converts*, not when it reaches. Judge channels by the funnel, not the feed.

Read `docs/crewtransition-strategic-advisor.md` in full before such work. It is the source of truth and is revised over time.

## Question bank — REQUIRED reading before touching any assessment question
Read `docs/crewtransition-question-audit-framework.md` in full before changing question stems, options, premises, order, or gating. It governs how to reframe questions toward worth/curiosity without altering what the engine measures.
